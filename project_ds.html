The "Hidden" Crisis in Healthcare Access - Identifying the Group that Falls through the Cracks

DubsTech Datathon machine learning project which leverages clustering methods to identify the demographics, geographic and socioeconomic status that falls through the cracks in healthcare.

Feburary 11, 2026 Maisie Liu

üéâ Last weekend, I had the opportunity to participate in the DubsTech Datathon with an incredible team, where we analyzed national healthcare survey data to uncover disparities in access to care. Within 24 hours, we worked with 23,000+ data points across 54 health indicators to better understand which populations face the greatest cost-related barriers.
My primary contribution focused on the machine learning component. We first identified key dimensions contributing to limited healthcare accessibility ‚Äî including cost, insurance status, behavioral factors, disability, and mental health indicators. We then built a K-means clustering model to group populations based on shared barrier patterns. By ranking clusters using a composite risk scoring approach, we identified the groups with the lowest overall rank scores ‚Äî highlighting populations most at risk of falling through the cracks despite having significant care needs.
